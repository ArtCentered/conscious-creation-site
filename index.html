The Book

We've been taught that consciousness is an accident. That we're passive observers in a mechanical universe. This worldview has left modern life feeling fragmented and purposeless.

What if we've had it backwards?

Part memoir, part philosophical framework, part practical toolkit, The Conscious Architect weaves personal narrative with evidence from neuroscience, quantum physics, and contemplative practice. A father's schizophrenia. The cost of coming out. A cat who became a teacher. A wedding dance holding secret grief. These stories carry a framework called Process Idealism and four tools for working with it. Vision, Intention, Action, and Perspective Alchemy.

The toolkit works whether or not you accept the philosophy. The philosophy is offered with honest caveats, never false certainty. And the story beneath both is one of a questioning child who was told the sacred had no room for him and spent thirty years finding it everywhere.

The Architect's Toolkit

Vision

The image you hold with sustained attention. What you consistently observe becomes more stable, more real. A childhood dream revisited only in passing fades. A calling returned to every single day crystallizes into a life.

Intention

The authentic foundation beneath the image. When your body believes, biology follows. A physical therapist once put it simply. "Find what your body believes will work."

Action

The practice that reshapes the instrument. The monks didn't achieve extraordinary states through belief alone. They practiced for 10,000 hours. Every deliberate choice sculpts the prism through which you experience reality.

Perspective Alchemy

The lens you choose to look through. The same encounter at a grocery store can feel like an irritation or an invitation, depending on your inner weather. The practice is choosing which interpretation becomes your lived reality.

Knudsen

Travis Knudsen is the Executive Director of the Lane Regional Air Protection Agency in Oregon, where he leads coalitions addressing contaminated neighborhoods and rural air quality. Before that, he spent a decade as a broadcast meteorologist, translating atmospheric chaos into clarity for television audiences. Both careers taught him the same skill. Recognizing patterns across complex systems and making the invisible visible.

The Conscious Architect grew from a personal crisis during the 2020 pandemic, when a collapsing sense of meaning led to a paintbrush, a philosophical framework, and eventually this book. It is part memoir, part practical toolkit, and part investigation into the deepest question Travis has carried since childhood. What is consciousness, and how does it shape reality?

He lives in Eugene, Oregon, with his husband Michael and their cat Zeus.
